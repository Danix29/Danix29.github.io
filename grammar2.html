Grammar Exercise 1: Rule Builder

Welcome to the first step in strengthening your English grammar! This section will guide you through exercises designed to help you understand key grammar rules, apply them correctly, and build accurate sentence structures with confidence.

Grammar Exercise: Rephrasing

Enhance your English grammar skills by rephrasing sentences. Express the same idea in your own words using passive, impersonal reporting, or causative structures.

Information

Rephrasing Exercise – Passive & Causative Structures

Rewrite the following sentences using the words in parentheses so the meaning stays the same.

1. The city council is building more affordable houses.
→ More affordable houses ___________________________________. (Passive)

2. People say that immigration has improved cultural diversity.
→ Immigration ___________________________________. (Impersonal passive)

3. The mayor asked an architect to redesign the entire neighborhood.
→ The mayor ___________________________________. (Causative)

4. Someone cleans the public parks every morning.
→ The public parks ___________________________________. (Passive)

5. It is believed that the government will invest more in public housing.
→ The government ___________________________________. (Personal passive)

6. They hired a team to renovate the abandoned building.
→ They ___________________________________. (Causative)

7. People think that crime rates have decreased in the suburbs.
→ It ___________________________________. (Impersonal passive)

8. They painted all the buildings in the community last month.
→ All the buildings ___________________________________. (Passive)

9. A local charity gave food to many immigrant families.
→ Food ___________________________________. (Passive)

10. The tenants paid a plumber to fix the broken pipes.
→ The tenants ___________________________________. (Causative)
