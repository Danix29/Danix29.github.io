Grammar Theory: Unit 4

1. Passive Voice

Used when the action is more important than the doer.
Structure: Object + to be (tense) + past participle (+ by + subject)
Example: My car was washed.

2. Impersonal Passive

Used for opinions, beliefs, or reports without specifying who says them.
Structures:
• It + to be + past participle + that…
• Subject + to be + past participle + to + infinitive
Example: It is thought that AI will replace jobs.

3. Passive Infinitive & Passive Gerund

Used in complex sentence structures.
Structures:
• to be + past participle (e.g., The city needs to be cleaned.)
• being + past participle (e.g., He dislikes being told what to do.)

4. Causative Forms

Used when someone arranges for another person to do something.
Structure: Subj + have/get + object + past participle
Example: She had her house painted.

These structures are essential for conveying actions when the focus is not on the doer, but on the process or result.

Unit 4: Living Together Vocabulary

Society – Vocabulary

Words:
1. Life Expectancy
• It is a compound noun that refers to the average number of years a person is expected to live based on statistical data. • Synonyms: Longevity, Life Span (esperanza de vida).
2. Population Growth
• It is a compound noun that refers to the increase in the number of people in a particular area over time. • Synonyms: Demographic Expansion, Population Increase (crecimiento poblacional).
3. Senior Citizens
• It is a compound noun that refers to elderly people, usually those who have retired. • Synonyms: Elderly, Pensioners (personas mayores).
4. End Poverty
• It is a verb phrase that refers to eliminating extreme financial hardship and lack of resources. • Synonyms: Eradicate Poverty, Alleviate Poverty (erradicar la pobreza).
5. Aging Population
• It is a compound noun that refers to a society where the proportion of elderly people is increasing. • Synonyms: Graying Society, Elderly Demographic (población envejecida).
6. Own a Property
• It is a verb phrase that refers to having legal possession of a house, land, or building. • Synonyms: Possess Real Estate, Be a Homeowner (poseer una propiedad).
7. Be Homeless
• It is a verb phrase that refers to lacking a permanent place to live. • Synonyms: Live on the Streets, Be Displaced (estar sin hogar).
8. A Tiny Minority
• It is a noun phrase that refers to a very small percentage of a population or group. • Synonyms: Small Fraction, Insignificant Group (una minoría muy pequeña).
9. Individual Privacy
• It is a compound noun that refers to a person’s right to keep their personal life and information protected from others. • Synonyms: Personal Confidentiality, Data Protection (privacidad individual).
10. Protect Identity
• It is a verb phrase that refers to safeguarding personal information from theft or misuse. • Synonyms: Secure Personal Information, Prevent Identity Theft (proteger la identidad).

Expressions with “Live” – Vocabulary


Expressions:
1. Live and Learn
• It is an expression that refers to constantly discovering new and sometimes surprising things through experience.
• Synonyms: Gain Experience, Learn from Mistakes (vivir y aprender).
2. Live and Let Live
• It is an expression that refers to accepting other people’s beliefs and ways of life, even if they are very different from one’s own.
• Synonyms: Be Tolerant, Respect Differences (vive y deja vivir).
3. Live for the Moment
• It is an expression that refers to enjoying the present and not worrying about the future.
• Synonyms: Seize the Day, Enjoy the Present (vivir el momento).
4. Live Happily Ever After
• It is an expression that refers to having a happy life forever, usually at the end of a story or fairyç tale.
• Synonyms: Have Eternal Happiness, Fairy Tale Ending (vivir felices para siempre).
5. Live in the Past
• It is an expression that refers to thinking and talking too much about past events rather than focusing on the present.
• Synonyms: Dwell on the Past, Be Nostalgic (vivir en el pasado).
6. Live the Dream
• It is an expression that refers to experiencing an ideal or highly desired life.
• Synonyms: Achieve One’s Goals, Have a Perfect Life (vivir el sueño).

Positive Adjectives – Vocabulary


Adjectives:
1. Appealing
• It is an adjective that refers to something attractive or interesting.
• Synonyms: Attractive, Inviting (atractivo).
2. Ambitious
• It is an adjective that refers to having a strong desire to succeed or achieve something.
• Synonyms: Determined, Aspiring (ambicioso).
3. Valuable
• It is an adjective that refers to something of great worth, importance, or usefulness.
• Synonyms: Precious, Worthwhile (valioso).
4. Frustrating
• It is an adjective that refers to something that causes annoyance or discouragement.
• Synonyms: Annoying, Disheartening (frustrante).
5. Well-Established
• It is an adjective that refers to something that has existed for a long time and is widely recognized or respected.
• Synonyms: Recognized, Long-Standing (bien establecido).
6. Efficient
• It is an adjective that refers to achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense.
• Synonyms: Effective, Productive (eficaz).
7. Convenient
• It is an adjective that refers to something easy to use or suitable for a particular purpose.
• Synonyms: Handy, Useful (conveniente).
8. Effortless
• It is an adjective that refers to something done with little or no effort.
• Synonyms: Easy, Smooth (sin esfuerzo).
9. Affordable
• It is an adjective that refers to something reasonably priced and not too expensive.
• Synonyms: Inexpensive, Cost-Effective (asequible).
10. Standard
• It is an adjective that refers to something widely accepted as a norm or requirement.
• Synonyms: Usual, Normal (estándar).

Reading – Vocabulary


Structure:
1. Essential
• It is an adjective that refers to something absolutely necessary or extremely important.
• Synonyms: Crucial, Vital (esencial).
2. Joy
• It is a noun that refers to a feeling of great happiness and pleasure.
• Synonyms: Happiness, Delight (alegría).
3. Instantly
• It is an adverb that refers to something happening immediately or without delay.
• Synonyms: Immediately, At Once (instantáneamente).
4. Unfamiliar
• It is an adjective that refers to something unknown or not recognized.
• Synonyms: Unknown, Strange (desconocido).
5. Infinite
• It is an adjective that refers to something without limits or endless.
• Synonyms: Boundless, Limitless (infinito).
6. Truly
• It is an adverb that refers to something genuine or real.
• Synonyms: Genuinely, Honestly (verdaderamente).
7. Join In
• It is a phrasal verb that refers to participating in an activity.
• Synonyms: Take Part, Participate (unirse).
8. Date Back To
• It is a phrasal verb that refers to something originating from a specific time in the past.
• Synonyms: Originate, Go Back To (datar de).
9. Line
• It is a phrasal verb that refers to arranging things or people in a row or preparing for something.
• Synonyms: Arrange, Align (alinearse).
